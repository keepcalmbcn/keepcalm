# Women's Volleyball - Growth & Marketing Strategy

How we attract women to Keep Calm Women's Volleyball and make them feel welcome enough to come back. This is the playbook behind the posts in this folder.

The goal is not just to broadcast that the group exists. It is to remove the specific reasons women hesitate, and replace them with reasons to show up.

---

## The barriers we are removing

Women told us, directly and through the community survey, what holds them back. Every piece of our marketing should answer at least one of these:

1. **"I'll be the only beginner and look silly."** > We say, every time: all levels welcome, beginners always there, no scorekeeping, teams rotate.
2. **"I don't want to turn up alone not knowing anyone."** > We name a clear meeting point and a named organiser, and we actively invite bring-a-friend.
3. **"It feels intimidating / too competitive / too male."** > Women-only framing, warm candid photos, "social not a tournament" language.
4. **"It'll be expensive or a commitment."** > It's free, no membership, no gear needed, come when you can.
5. **"Is it safe?"** > Public beach, daytime sessions, named organiser, 18+ adults-only community with clear policies.

If a post does not visibly answer one of these, rewrite it.

---

## The hooks that work for volleyball

- **Free + beach + sunshine.** Volleyball's biggest advantage over padel: zero cost and an iconic Barcelona setting. Lead with this.
- **The social half.** "The sport is the excuse." Post-game drinks on the sand is half the draw - sell the friendships, not the spikes.
- **Forgiving format.** Beach volleyball is genuinely beginner-friendly. Say so.
- **Low friction.** No booking a court, no racket, no fee. "Just show up" is a real and rare selling point.

---

## Channels (in priority order)

1. **Within Keep Calm first.** Cross-post to the mixed volleyball group, the main community channel, and the women's padel group - these women already trust us and are the warmest leads.
2. **Instagram** - feed post + stories. Reels of real sessions (golden hour, laughing, a beginner landing a hit) outperform static graphics.
3. **Facebook groups** - Barcelona expat / women in Barcelona / English-speakers groups. Follow each group's self-promo rules.
4. **Meetup.com** - listed event, free to attend, captures people actively searching for things to do.
5. **Reddit** - r/Barcelona, r/expats_barcelona. Keep it personal and honest, not an advert (see reddit.md).

> Note: when cross-posting to **external** communities that are not ours, keep it neutral and avoid heavy Keep Calm branding where the host group asks for it - respect their rules.

---

## Tactics that actually pull women in

- **Bring-a-friend is the single biggest lever.** Women come and stay when they arrive with someone. Make "bring a friend" a standing line in every post and verbally at every session.
- **Women ambassadors.** A friendly woman who greets newcomers by name at the meeting point converts first-timers into regulars better than any caption.
- **First-timer shout-outs.** Welcome new faces in the group (with their consent) so others see newcomers are normal and celebrated.
- **Partner with women's communities in Barcelona.** Women-in-Barcelona / expat-women networks are a perfect audience. Offer a friendly women-only taster, not a hard sell.
- **Consistency beats blitz.** A regular, reliable women's slot people can count on builds trust faster than a one-off launch push.
- **Close the loop.** After a good session, post a warm photo and a "next one is..." - momentum is the best advert.

---

## Content rhythm (lightweight)

- **Before:** session announcement in WhatsApp + a story. Reshare 1-2 days out.
- **After:** one warm candid photo or short reel, tag the vibe, point to the next session.
- **Monthly:** one "join the women's group" push across Instagram + a Barcelona women's Facebook group.

Keep it sustainable - a steady trickle is better than a burst that fizzles.

---

## What good looks like (rough signals)

- New women joining the WhatsApp group month on month
- First-timers returning for a second session (the real test)
- Women bringing friends
- Women stepping up to host or organise - the ultimate sign it's working, and the path to the group outlasting any one person

---

## Tone reminders

- Warm, plain, human. No corporate or salesy language.
- All-levels-welcome and women-only must be unmistakable in every asset.
- Never overstate - if a session is small and chilled, say so. Honesty is the brand.
- No em dashes anywhere (community style rule).
