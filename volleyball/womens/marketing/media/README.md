# Media Guide - Keep Calm Women's Volleyball

This folder is for photos and visuals used in marketing the women's volleyball activity. The beach setting is a huge asset - lean into it.

## What Works Well

### Action and play
- Candid in-rally moments - a dig, a set, a jump at the net, not posed
- A beginner getting a clean hit - the look of "I actually did that"
- Group celebrations after a point - arms up, laughing
- Both sides of the net in frame to show it's a real, social game

### People and group moments
- A relaxed women's group shot on the sand after a session
- Players chatting and laughing between rallies
- Post-game social - sitting on the sand, drinks in hand
- A diverse group showing the international, all-ages, all-levels crowd

### Setting and atmosphere
- Wide shots with the sea, sand, and Barceloneta backdrop
- Golden-hour afternoon light (the sessions catch lovely late sun)
- The group setting up the net together - shows it's welcoming and hands-on

## What to Avoid
- Overly posed or stiff group photos
- Anything that looks competitive or intense rather than a friendly session
- Shots that make it look intimidating for beginners - this puts people off
- Empty or staged shots with no people
- Low-res, muddy, or harshly backlit midday shots
- Any image that doesn't make the women-only, all-levels welcome feel clear

## Best Angles for Barcelona Audience
The draw here is friendly, accessible, women-only beach volleyball where every level belongs. Photos that feel warm and relaxed - "you'd fit right in here on a first try" - are more compelling than anything that looks competitive or exclusive. Make sure visuals clearly reflect a women-only group of mixed ability having fun.

## Formats Needed
- Square (1:1) - Instagram feed
- Portrait (4:5) - Instagram feed, best for mobile
- Landscape (16:9) - Facebook, Meetup cover images
- Stories (9:16) - Instagram and WhatsApp stories
