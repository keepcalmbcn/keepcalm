# Media Guide - Keep Calm Volleyball

Photos and visuals that work well for promoting volleyball. The beach setting is a huge asset - lean into it.

## Best Shots to Capture

**Action shots**
- Mid-rally moments: someone jumping to spike or block
- Serves and digs with the net clearly visible
- Group celebrations after a point - arms up, laughing

**Setting and atmosphere**
- Wide shots showing the beach backdrop (sea, palm trees, Barceloneta skyline)
- The group warming up or setting up the net on the sand
- Golden hour light during the Saturday afternoon session (14:00-18:00 catches great afternoon sun)

**People-focused**
- Mixed skill levels playing together - a beginner attempting a dig is just as good as a pro spike
- Post-game social: people sitting on the sand, chatting, drinks in hand
- Diverse group shots showing the international, all-ages crowd

**Seasonal variety**
- Winter beach volleyball - empty beach, moody sky, still playing
- Summer crowded beach atmosphere with the game going on

## What to Avoid
- Overly competitive or intense expressions - this is social, not a tournament
- Empty or staged shots with no people
- Poor light or muddy colours (avoid midday harsh sun for portraits)

## Formats Needed
- Square (1:1) - Instagram feed
- Portrait (4:5) - Instagram feed, best for mobile
- Landscape (16:9) - Facebook, Meetup cover images
- Stories (9:16) - Instagram and WhatsApp stories
