# FAQ - Coastal Hike & Beach (Garraf to Sitges)

Quick answers to the questions we get asked most. Anything not covered here? Drop a message in the hiking WhatsApp group: https://keepcalm.fit

---

**How do I sign up?**
There's no form this time. We've posted this as a WhatsApp Event in the hiking group - just tap "Going" so we have a rough headcount. That's it.

**Can I bring my dog?**
Yes! Dogs are very welcome. This route has a few climbs and some rocky sections, so keep your dog close, especially on the uphill bits and the descents into Sitges.

**Can I bring my boyfriend, husband, partner or a friend?**
Absolutely - everyone's welcome. Bring whoever you'd like along, the more the merrier. It helps the headcount if they mark themselves as "Going" on the event too.

**Is it just walking, or is it a proper technical hike?**
It's walking, but a bit more of a workout than our flat coastal walks. There's about 300-400m of climbing spread across the route, up to the Ermita de la Trinitat and over a couple of headlands. Nothing technical, but you'll feel the hills. If you're comfortable with a roughly 3 to 3.5 hour walk with some uphill, you'll be fine.

**How much does it cost?**
The hike itself is completely free. The only cost is your train ticket - around €4-5 one way (it may be covered by your T-Mobilitat card).

**Do I need to be fit?**
A reasonable level of fitness helps. It's about 9 km over roughly 3 to 3.5 hours with some climbs, so it's more demanding than a flat beach walk. Just check you're comfortable with that before the day.

**Do I have to swim?**
Not at all. The swim is at the end on Sitges beach, so it's there for anyone who fancies a dip while we have lunch. Bring swimwear and a towel if you'd like to join in.

**Where do we eat?**
Bring a packed lunch and a snack to share. We'll have our shared lunch on Sitges beach at the end of the walk, then find somewhere for drinks and food. Bring some snacks for energy during the walk too, as lunch is on the later side.

**Can I leave early or head back on my own?**
Yes. Once we reach Sitges, return trains run regularly and take around 40 minutes back to Sants, so you're free to head back earlier or stay on later.

**What happens if the weather is bad?**
We'd postpone and announce it in the hiking WhatsApp group, so please make sure you've joined. We'll send an update either way the evening before.
