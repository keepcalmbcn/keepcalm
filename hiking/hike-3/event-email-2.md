ON THE DAY
------------------------------------------------------------

We'll be walking the GR-92 Camí de Ronda from Vilanova to Sitges - about 8.5 km of stunning coastal scenery with clifftop paths, coves, sea caves, and historic landmarks along the way.

Halfway through (maybe closer to the end) we'll stop at one of the beaches for a swim and snacks. We'll also try to put up a couple of volleyball nets, so look forward to a game on the sand!

Please take all your rubbish with you when we leave the beach - let's keep these beautiful spots clean for everyone who comes after us.

We expect to arrive in Sitges around 14:30-15:00, where we'll find somewhere for drinks and food before heading back.

We expect to be ready to leave Sitges between 15:00 and 16:00, though of course you're welcome to head back earlier or stay on later - return trains run regularly and take around 40 minutes back to Sants.

WHAT TO BRING & QUESTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

*What should I bring?*
- Water (1.5-2L - it gets warm in May, and even more so in the sunshine)
- Sun cream and hat (a must with the sunny forecast!)
- Trainers or hiking shoes (both are fine, hiking shoes are better on rocky sections)
- Swimwear and a towel for the mid-hike swim
- A packed lunch/snack to share at the beach stop
- A light jacket or extra layer (it can feel cool by the sea, especially after a swim)

*Can I bring my dog?*
Yes! Dogs are very welcome. Please keep your dog on a lead for the whole route though - several sections run right alongside an active railway line, and with a big group of us walking together a lead keeps your dog safe and comfortable around everyone.

*Can I bring my partner or a friend/s?*
Absolutely - everyone's welcome. Bring whoever you'd like along, the more the merrier. If they haven't signed up via the form yet, it helps us with the headcount if they do.

*Is the hike difficult?*
Not at all - it's extremely beginner-friendly. It's a very easy walk, almost entirely flat, so as long as you're happy being on your feet for a few hours you'll have no trouble at all.

*How much does the hike cost?*
Completely free - but please do consider bringing snacks/beverages to share amongst all your Keep Calm friends!
