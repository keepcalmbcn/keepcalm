# Media Guide - Keep Calm Hiking

This folder is for photos and visuals used in marketing the hiking activity.

## What Works Well

### Landscape and scenery
- Coastal clifftop trails with sea views (the Garraf coast is a standout)
- Mountain ridgelines with Barcelona or the Mediterranean visible in the distance
- Forest paths with dappled light
- Medieval villages and stone paths
- Panoramic summit shots

### People and group moments
- Candid walking shots - a loose line of people on a trail, not posed
- The shared lunch spread laid out on a rock or picnic blanket
- Small groups chatting during a rest stop
- A wide group shot at a viewpoint or summit
- Candid laughter and conversation mid-hike

### Details
- Boots on a rocky trail
- Backpacks and layers - showing the authentic, practical side
- Train platforms at early morning departure (Arc de Triomf, Plaça Espanya)
- Trail signs and Komoot route screenshots for announcements

## What to Avoid
- Overly posed or stiff group photos
- Anything that looks like a promotional shoot rather than a real day out
- Photos where people look exhausted or uncomfortable - this puts people off
- Low-res or heavily filtered shots

## Best Angles for Barcelona Audience
Catalunya has spectacular scenery within an hour of the city by train. Photos that make that proximity feel accessible - "this is a Sunday morning from Barcelona" - are more compelling than photos that look like a serious expedition.
