# Keep Calm Ski/Snowboard - Group Rules

Welcome to Keep Calm Ski/Snowboard! To keep things safe and enjoyable for everyone, please follow these guidelines.

> **These rules add to — but don't replace — our community-wide policies. Please read these first if you haven't already:**
> - [WhatsApp Rules](https://github.com/keepcalmbcn/keepcalm/blob/main/keep-calm/rules/whatsapp-rules.md) — group communication, unsolicited DMs, referral links, spam
> - [Community Conduct](https://github.com/keepcalmbcn/keepcalm/blob/main/keep-calm/rules/community-conduct.md) — respect, harassment, advertising, conflict resolution
> - [Event Rules](https://github.com/keepcalmbcn/keepcalm/blob/main/keep-calm/rules/event-rules.md) — commitment, cancellations, the strike system

## 1. Commitment

- If you commit to a trip, follow through
- Others may book accommodation, transport, or lift passes based on your attendance
- If your plans change, let the group know as early as possible

## 2. Safety

- Ski and snowboard at your own risk - know your limits
- Stick to runs that match your ability level
- Wear a helmet (strongly recommended)
- Follow the mountain's safety rules and signage
- Let someone know if you're going off on your own
- Have emergency contacts accessible on your phone

## 3. Logistics

- Organise your own transport and accommodation unless a group option is arranged
- Coordinate carpooling in the group - share costs fairly
- Be on time for agreed meeting points and departure times
- Don't hold the group up

## 4. Costs

- Ski trips aren't cheap - be upfront about shared costs (petrol, accommodation, passes)
- Settle up promptly after the trip
- If you drop out and others have booked based on your commitment, be prepared to cover your share

## 5. Be Sound

- Look out for each other on the mountain
