# Media Guide - Keep Calm Ski/Snowboard

Photos and visuals that work well for marketing this activity.

## Best Angles

The proximity of the Pyrenees to Barcelona is the killer hook. Most people don't realise you can wake up in the city, drive two hours, and be on the slopes by late morning. Visuals that reinforce this are gold.

## Photo Types to Prioritise

**The journey angle**
- Views of snowy mountains from the car or roadside - the contrast of palm trees or city behind you and snow ahead
- The moment you arrive and see the slopes for the first time
- Car full of friends with ski gear piled in

**On the slopes**
- Mixed-ability group shots on the mountain - show that it's genuinely all levels
- Candid moments: people laughing after a fall, waiting at the chairlift, high-fiving at the bottom
- Wide shots showing the scale of the resort (especially Grandvalira)
- First-timer moments - someone getting up for the first time

**The social side**
- Lunch on a sunny terrace with mountains in the background
- Group photos at the end of the day, gear still on, big smiles
- Evening meals or drinks after skiing - the community feel

**Graphic/design assets**
- Simple text-on-snow graphics: "Barcelona to the Pyrenees - 2 hours"
- Before/after: city skyline vs. snowy slopes
- Map graphics showing Barcelona's proximity to Grandvalira and Masella/La Molina

## Key Messages to Reinforce Visually

- Snow is genuinely close to Barcelona
- All levels welcome - not an elite or intimidating group
- It's a social trip as much as a ski trip
- Volunteer-run, no-profit community vibe
