# Friday Badminton - FAQ

## About the Session

### What is Keep Calm Badminton?
A weekly badminton session organised through the Keep Calm and Carry On community - a not-for-profit community for English speakers in Barcelona. We play every Friday afternoon at La Mar Bella Municipal Sports Complex, in partnership with **BCN Badminton**, who book the courts, run the games, and handle the RSVPs.

### Who is this for?
Everyone. It's a **mixed** game - ladies and gents both welcome, all ages - currently pitched at a **lower-intermediate** level. This is social badminton, not a competition, so turning up and having fun matters far more than the score.

### Is it beginner friendly?
The Friday session is aimed at a **lower-intermediate** level, so it suits people who know the basics and can hold a rally. Genuine beginners are still welcome to come along, but be honest about your level when you RSVP so the games stay enjoyable for everyone. If you're brand new, mention it in the group and we'll point you to the right session.

### How does BCN Badminton fit in?
We've partnered with **BCN Badminton** to run the Friday session. They book the courts, organise the doubles games, provide an experienced host on-site (Mel), and manage RSVPs and payment through Meetup. Keep Calm points the community to the session, and BCN Badminton handles the logistics on the day.

## The Weekly Session

### When and where do you play?
**Every Friday, 16:00 to 18:30** (2.5 hours) at **La Mar Bella Municipal Sports Complex**, Av. del Litoral, 86-96, Poblenou. See [Venue](friday-badminton-venues.md) for full details and how to get there.

### What format do you play?
**Doubles practice games** (2v2) across **4 courts** for **24 players**. Players rotate so everyone gets plenty of court time and games with different partners.

### How much does it cost and how do I pay?
**5€ per person**, which covers the court hire and shuttlecocks, paid to the host on the day. Bring the **exact amount** in cash, as the host won't have change. You can also pay by **Bizum or bank transfer** - ask the host for details.

### How many spots are there?
**24 players** per session. When it's full, Meetup runs an **automatic waitlist**, so if someone drops out the next person on the list is moved up automatically.

## Joining a Session

### How do I join?
1. Find the Friday session on [BCN Badminton's Meetup](https://www.meetup.com/barcelona-badminton/)
2. RSVP **yes** to claim your spot (or join the waitlist if it's full)
3. Bring your racket, indoor court shoes, water, and the exact cash
4. Turn up at La Mar Bella before the 16:00 start

### What if the session is full?
RSVP to join the **waitlist**. Meetup moves you up automatically if someone cancels, so it's always worth adding yourself.

### What if I can't make it after I RSVP?
Change your RSVP **as soon as possible** so a waitlisted player can take your spot. If you change your RSVP **within 24 hours** of the event, it counts as a **no-show** unless someone takes your place. People often cancel late assuming others want the spot - please don't, change it early.

## On the Day

### What should I bring?
- **Your own racket** (ask in the group if you need to borrow one)
- **Indoor court shoes** with non-marking soles
- **Water** to stay hydrated
- The **exact cash** (5€) if you're not paying by Bizum or transfer

### Do I need my own racket?
Bring your own if you have one. If you need to borrow one, ask in the group beforehand - members sometimes have a spare. Don't rely on rackets being available at the venue.

### What time should I arrive?
Aim to arrive a few minutes before **16:00** so you're warmed up and ready when court time starts.

### Where exactly are the courts?
In the main building, usually Courts 9 & 10 and 6 & 7. Signage can be confusing, so check the Meetup event for the court numbers and look for the group if you're unsure. See [Venue](friday-badminton-venues.md).

## Safety and Etiquette

### Is it safe? Who's responsible?
Participation is **voluntary and at your own risk**. The host facilitates the session but is not responsible for participants' wellbeing or for any lost or damaged belongings. Warm up before playing, stretch afterwards, and keep your valuables with you.

### What's expected of me on court?
Rotate so everyone gets a game, keep the banter friendly and inclusive, welcome newcomers, and help set up and pack down the nets and equipment. Respect the venue staff and other users of the sports hall.

## Weather

### What happens if it rains?
Nothing - it's **indoor**, so the session runs rain or shine.
