# Social Organiser - One-Pager

**The role in one sentence:** most plans in this group run themselves - members post "who's in?" and go - your job is the ones that need a helping hand: bigger events, paid events, and keeping the group's posting rules working.

*You're a volunteer, same as everyone else who helps Keep Calm run - this page is guardrails so we're roughly on the same page, not a checklist you're marked against. We're all doing this to grow the community together and meet great people along the way.*

## Rhythm

There's no weekly slot - Social runs on whatever members propose, as they propose it:

- **Casual invites** ("who wants dinner Saturday?"): members post these themselves, no organiser needed
- **When someone floats a bigger or paid idea** (a festival, a day trip, a market): turn it into a proper post with the details and a way to confirm numbers
- **When someone asks to share an external link, flyer, or Meetup/Instagram post**: you're the approval step per the [group rules](rules.md)

## Handled automatically (you do nothing)

- Nothing here is automated - Social is member-driven by design, and official posts are yours when they're needed

## Yours to do

- **Turn interest into an event**: once a casual suggestion gets traction, post it properly with time, place, cost, and how to confirm
- **Gatekeep external content**: approve (or decline) external event links, flyers, and Meetup/Instagram posts before they go in the group, per the [group rules](rules.md)
- **Watch for commercial activity**: no buying/selling, no promoting other communities - flag and remove per the rules
- **Answer questions** in the group about a planned event
- **Welcome newcomers** and encourage them to jump into whatever's happening
- **Day-of calls** for anything you've turned into an official event: weather, timing, headcount

## You decide without asking anyone

- Whether a casual suggestion is ready to become an official post
- Approving or declining external links, flyers, and outside-event shares
- Format and logistics for the events you organise directly

## Decide together with other organisers

- Recurring paid partnerships (e.g. a regular venue discount or festival tie-in)
- Anything that looks like it needs its own budget or contract
- Collaborations with other Barcelona communities/Meetup groups

## Escalate to the Community Lead only for

- Money beyond the above
- Bans and appeals
- Partnership contract changes
- Anything technical: automation, the website

## Handover

Stepping down? Follow the Stepping Down checklist in the [Organiser Guide](../keep-calm/governance/organiser-guide.md#stepping-down).
