## Your First Week

### Step 1: Join a WhatsApp Group

You've probably already done this! If not, find the sport you're interested in at [keepcalm.fit](https://keepcalm.fit) and join the WhatsApp group.

### Step 2: Watch for the Poll

Each sport posts a weekly poll asking who can make the next session. When you see it:
- Vote for the time slots that work for you
- Only vote if you genuinely plan to come
- Voting = committing to show up

### Step 3: Check the Games Post

After the poll closes, organisers will post who's playing when. Look for:
- Your name and assigned time/court
- Whether you're the "booker" (you'll book the court) or a player (you'll join someone else's booking)
- Any special instructions

### Step 4: Show Up and Play!

- Arrive on time (or a few minutes early)
- Introduce yourself‚Äîeveryone knows what it's like to be new
- Have fun!

## The Rules (Quick Version)

Full rules are in [Rules.md](../rules/rules.md), but here's the short version:

### Show Up If You Commit
- If you vote in a poll, you're saying you'll be there
- If plans change, give as much notice as possible
- Help find a replacement if you need to cancel

### Be Respectful
- Welcome others, especially newcomers
- Keep WhatsApp on-topic and positive
- No advertising or self-promotion

### The Strike System
- Repeated no-shows or bad behaviour ‚Üí strikes
- 3 strikes ‚Üí removal from the community
- Don't worry‚Äîjust be reliable and you'll be fine

## Tips for Your First Session

### Before
- Check the weather (especially for outdoor sports)
- Bring water and appropriate clothing
- Know where you're going (save the venue address)
- Have the booker's contact if needed

### During
- Introduce yourself: "Hi, I'm [name], this is my first time"
- Don't be shy‚Äîeveryone's been new
- If you don't understand something, ask
- Focus on having fun, not being perfect

### After
- Stick around for post-game drinks if you can (great way to meet people)
- Say thanks to the organiser
- Let us know how it went‚Äîwe love feedback!

## Making Friends

Sports are the excuse. Friendships are the point.

**Ways to connect:**
- Stay for drinks after sessions
- Chat during breaks and between games
- Try a different sport‚Äîyou'll meet different people
- Come to social events
- Be a regular‚Äîfamiliarity builds friendship

**It takes time.** Your first session might feel awkward. By session 5, you'll recognise faces. By session 10, you'll have favourites. Keep showing up.

## Common Questions

### What if I don't know anyone?
That's exactly why we exist. Everyone's been there. Just show up‚Äîyou'll meet people.

### What if I'm terrible at the sport?
We have all levels. Vote honestly about your skill level and you'll be matched appropriately. No one expects perfection.

### What if I can't make it after signing up?
Post in the group ASAP, help find a replacement if you can, and let the organiser know. Life happens‚Äîjust communicate.

### What if I want to try a different sport?
Go for it! Join the WhatsApp group for that sport and vote in the next poll.

### What if I have a problem with someone?
Talk to an organiser privately. We take community health seriously. See [Community-Conduct.md](../rules/community-conduct.md).

### What if I want to bring a friend?
Great! Have them join the WhatsApp group and vote in the poll. Don't just show up with extras without checking first.
