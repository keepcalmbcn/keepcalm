# Keep Calm Core

Core is the backbone of Keep Calm: the members who have stuck around, show up across our activities, give back, and help the community grow. They are the steady, familiar faces, not people passing through Barcelona for a season.

This document defines what Core is, how someone becomes Core, and the standard Core members are held to. For the message we send when inviting someone, see [invite.md](invite.md).

---

## What Core is

Core is an invite-only group for the people at the heart of the community. It isn't tied to any one sport. It brings together the members who keep showing up across all our activities, so they can build real friendships, stay in touch beyond individual events, and help shape where Keep Calm goes next.

In a community with hundreds of members and new faces every week, it can be hard to get past a quick hello. Core is a calmer, smaller space for the people who are genuinely invested in each other and in the community.

## What Core is not

- It is not about status or being "better" than anyone.
- It is not a reward you apply for and tick off. Core members are usually already obvious to everyone.
- It does not require a title or formal role. Reliable, everyday contribution is enough.

---

## How you become Core

Core is earned over time, then confirmed by the organiser team. Four things describe a Core member. They are a guide for a judgement, not a rigid scorecard, but all four should genuinely be true.

### 1. Time ⏳
An active member for **6+ months**. Core members are familiar faces, not newcomers, and not just here for a season.

### 2. Participation 🎾
Has joined at least **5 Keep Calm events** across any sport or social category. Core is cross-community, so breadth counts as much as frequency.

### 3. Contribution 🙌
Has given back in a real way. That can be organising or co-organising an event, but it does not have to be. Consistent informal help counts just as much:

- Welcoming and onboarding newcomers
- Setup and cleanup at sessions
- Bringing or lending equipment
- Helping with Spanish or Catalan venue communications
- Taking photos for the community

A title is not required. Reliable help is. See [Informal Contributions](../governance/role-descriptions.md#informal-contributions).

### 4. Conduct 💛
Treats people well and is someone the community trusts. Respected by other members, and with no unresolved conduct issues or active strikes. This is the bar that matters most, and it is the one that keeps applying after someone joins (see [The Core standard](#the-core-standard)).

---

## Endorsement and review

Core is confirmed by the organiser team. In practice it is rarely a surprise, because Core members are already well known.

The normal starting point is an existing Core member or organiser vouching for someone. The organisers then confirm against the four pillars above. We deliberately do not require a fixed number of referrals: that can't work before Core exists, and it risks turning Core into a clique, which runs against our inclusive values.

If someone clearly fits but no one has put their name forward, anyone can raise them with the organisers.

## Founding cohort

The first Core members are invited directly by the organisers to seed the group. Once that founding cohort exists, the criteria and endorsement process above apply for everyone after them.

---

## The Core standard

Becoming Core is not a one-time gate. Because Core members represent Keep Calm and help set its tone, more is expected of them, not less. The community and its members deserve respect, and Core is where that is held to the highest standard.

Core members are expected to:

- Treat every member and guest with respect, especially newcomers
- Be reliable: show up when they commit, give notice when they can't
- Help resolve problems calmly rather than add to them
- Uphold the [Community Conduct](../policies/community-conduct.md) rules and set the example

## Losing Core status

Core status is ongoing, not permanent. Serious or repeated conduct breaches, as handled under [Community Conduct](../policies/community-conduct.md) and the strike system, can mean leaving Core. Removal from Core is an organiser decision, handled privately and with the chance to be heard, in line with [Decision Making](../governance/decision-making.md#appeals).

This is the heart of accountability: Core is a position of trust, and the trust has to be kept.

Use this when announcing or explaining Core in the community channels. It uses WhatsApp `*bold*` formatting.

```
_What does it take to become a Core Member?_

Core Members are the backbone of Keep Calm - the people who show up, contribute, and help the community grow. Core isn't about status or being "better" than anyone. It's recognition of the people who've stuck around and given their time.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

*01 - Time* ⏳
Be an active member for *6+ months*. Core Members are familiar faces, not newcomers.

*02 - Participation* 🎾
Join at least *5 Keep Calm events* across any sport or social category.

*03 - Contribution* 🙌
Give back in a real way - organise or co-organise an event, or be one of the people who reliably helps out (welcoming newcomers, setup, kit, photos). No title needed.

*04 - Conduct* 💛
Treat people well and be someone the community trusts. Core Members are held to a higher bar, because they represent Keep Calm.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

📌 _Core is by invitation and confirmed by the organiser team. If that sounds like you, odds are we already know it._
```
