# Decision Making

This document explains how decisions are made in Keep Calm, who has authority over what, and how to propose changes. Clear governance prevents conflicts and makes the community more sustainable.

---

## Guiding Principles

### Decisions Should Be

- **Transparent**: People understand why decisions were made
- **Proportionate**: Authority matches responsibility
- **Consistent**: Similar situations handled similarly
- **Inclusive**: Affected people have input where practical
- **Documented**: Important decisions are recorded

### We Avoid

- Decisions by a single person that affect everyone
- Changing things without telling people
- Endless discussion without resolution
- Overriding people in their areas of responsibility

---

## Decision Levels

### Level 1: Individual Decisions
**Made by:** Any organiser within their scope
**Examples:** Game assignments, booker selection, minor schedule adjustments
**Process:** Just do it, inform affected people
**Approval needed:** None

### Level 2: Sport-Level Decisions
**Made by:** Sport Lead(s)
**Examples:** Venue changes, session timing, format adjustments, new policies for that sport
**Process:** Discuss with co-leads, inform participants, implement
**Approval needed:** None (inform core team of significant changes)

### Level 3: Community-Wide Decisions
**Made by:** Core Team
**Examples:** New sports, community policies, removing members, partnerships, financial decisions
**Process:** Discussion, proposal, core team agreement
**Approval needed:** Core team consensus or majority

### Level 4: Foundational Decisions
**Made by:** Core Team with community input
**Examples:** Major policy changes, legal structure (asociación), fundamental value changes
**Process:** Proposal, community feedback, core team decision
**Approval needed:** Core team consensus

## Who Decides What

### Sport Leads Decide

**Full authority over:**
- Who plays in which session/court
- Booker assignments
- Minor schedule adjustments
- Welcoming/onboarding for their sport
- Day-to-day problem solving
- Strike recommendations (for their sport)

**Authority with coordination:**
- Venue changes (inform core team)
- Session timing changes (inform affected members)
- Format changes (inform affected members)
- New sub-events (beginner sessions, etc.)

**Needs core team approval:**
- Adding fees where there were none
- Removing the sport entirely
- Partnerships that affect other sports
- Anything that affects Keep Calm reputation

### Core Team Decides

**Full authority over:**
- New sports/activities
- Community-wide policies
- Strike/removal escalations
- Conflict resolution (escalated)
- External partnerships
- Financial decisions
- Website and social media strategy
- Community direction and vision

**With community input:**
- Major policy changes
- Significant structural changes
- Changes that affect everyone

**Needs careful consideration:**
- Anything that could divide the community
- Changes to fundamental values
- Legal/formal structure changes

### Everyone Decides

**By participating:**
- What activities you join
- Feedback on what's working and not
- Whether to volunteer

**By voting (when asked):**
- Community preferences surveys
- Feedback on proposed changes
- Input on new directions

## The Core Team

### What It Is

A small group (3-5 people) responsible for community-wide decisions and coordination across sports.

### Current Responsibilities

- Monthly coordination meeting
- Resolving escalated conflicts
- Approving new sports/major changes
- Setting community policies
- Supporting sport leads
- Representing Keep Calm externally

### How Decisions Are Made

**For routine matters:**
- Any core team member can decide
- Inform others after
- Others can raise concerns

**For significant matters:**
- Discussion in core team chat/meeting
- Seek consensus where possible
- If no consensus: majority vote
- Document the decision and reasoning

**For contentious matters:**
- Ensure all perspectives are heard
- Take time if needed
- Aim for consensus
- If voting: clear majority required
- Those who disagree still support the decision publicly

### Conflict of Interest

If a core team member has personal interest in a decision:
- Declare the conflict
- Recuse from the decision (or)
- Let others know and let them weigh accordingly

## Making Proposals

### Who Can Propose Changes

Anyone in the community can propose changes. The process depends on the scope.

### Small Changes (Sport-Level)

**Process:**
1. Talk to the Sport Lead
2. Discuss informally
3. Lead decides whether to implement
4. Lead informs affected members

**Examples:**
- Different warm-up time
- New venue suggestion
- Format tweak

### Medium Changes (Cross-Sport or Policy)

**Process:**
1. Write up the proposal (what, why, how)
2. Share with relevant Sport Lead(s) or Core Team
3. Discussion period (1-2 weeks)
4. Decision by appropriate authority
5. Announce to affected groups

**Examples:**
- New event type (cross-sport social)
- New rule for all sports
- New communication tool

### Large Changes (Foundational)

**Process:**
1. Detailed written proposal
2. Core team initial review
3. Community feedback period (2-4 weeks)
4. Revise based on feedback
5. Core team decision
6. Announce to community with reasoning

**Examples:**
- Registering as asociación
- Major policy change
- Adding/removing core values
- Significant structural change

## Handling Disagreements

### Between Members

1. Try to resolve directly
2. Involve Sport Lead if needed
3. Escalate to Core Team if unresolved
4. See [Community-Conduct.md](community-conduct.md)

### Between Members and Organisers

1. Discuss privately with the organiser
2. If unresolved, raise with Sport Lead (or Core Team if it's the Lead)
3. Core Team makes final call
4. Accept the decision gracefully

### Between Organisers

1. Discuss directly between those involved
2. If same sport: work it out or involve Core Team
3. If different sports: Core Team mediates
4. Focus on what's best for the community

### Between Sport Leads

1. Discuss directly
2. Involve Core Team if needed
3. Core Team decision is final

### With Core Team Decisions

1. You can express disagreement respectfully
2. Core Team should explain reasoning
3. Once decided, the community moves forward together
4. Ongoing disagreement handled through proper channels, not public conflict

## Transparency

### What We Share

- Major decisions and reasoning
- Policy changes
- New initiatives
- Changes that affect members

### How We Share

- WhatsApp announcements for immediate impact
- Documentation updates for reference
- Direct communication for affected individuals

### What We Don't Share

- Private conflict details
- Strike/disciplinary specifics (privacy)
- Internal disagreements during decision process
- Sensitive personal information

## Appealing Decisions

### When You Can Appeal

- Strikes or disciplinary actions against you
- Removal from groups or events
- Decisions that significantly affect you personally

### How to Appeal

1. Contact a core team member not involved in original decision
2. Explain why you think the decision should be reconsidered
3. Provide any new information or context
4. Core Team reviews and decides
5. Their decision is final

### Not Grounds for Appeal

- Disagreeing with game assignments
- Not liking a policy that applies to everyone
- Personality conflicts with organisers
- Decisions within organiser's normal authority

## Emergency Decisions

Sometimes decisions can't wait for normal process.

### Who Can Make Them

- Sport Lead for their sport
- Any Core Team member for community-wide

### When Appropriate

- Safety issues
- Immediate conflict requiring intervention
- Time-sensitive opportunities
- System failures

### Process

1. Make the decision
2. Document what you decided and why
3. Inform other relevant people ASAP
4. Review later to confirm or adjust

### Examples

- Cancelling an event due to dangerous weather
- Removing someone from a session for aggressive behaviour
- Accepting a last-minute venue change

## Adding New Sports or Activities

### Proposal Requirements

Before proposing a new sport, have answers for:
- What is the activity?
- Who would organise it?
- When would it run?
- Where (venue options)?
- What's the cost model?
- Is there demand?
- How does it fit Keep Calm values?

### Decision Process

1. Submit proposal to Core Team
2. Core Team reviews feasibility and fit
3. Discussion with proposer
4. Decision (approve, modify, defer, decline)
5. If approved: setup support and launch

### Criteria

We consider:
- Alignment with Keep Calm values
- Demand from community
- Feasibility (venues, equipment, etc.)
- Organiser commitment and capability
- Resource requirements
- Overlap with existing activities

## Financial Decisions

### Current State

Keep Calm is informal and doesn't hold significant funds. Members pay venues directly.

### If Money Is Involved

**Small amounts** (equipment, supplies):
- Sport Lead can approve for their sport
- Document expenses
- Community contributions are voluntary

**Larger amounts** (events, trips):
- Core Team approval needed
- Clear budget and cost-sharing
- Transparent accounting

**If We Formalise** (asociación):
- Formal financial policies required
- Designated treasurer role
- Annual accounting
- This document will be updated

## Changing This Document

Changes to decision-making processes are foundational.

**Process:**
1. Proposal with reasoning
2. Core Team review
3. Community feedback opportunity
4. Core Team decision
5. Document updated
6. Community informed
