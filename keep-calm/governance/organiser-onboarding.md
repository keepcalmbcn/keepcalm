# Organiser Onboarding - Expectations, Benefits, and Messages

Everything for bringing a new organiser on board: the one-pager we send them **before they say yes** (so nothing comes as a shock later), the invitation message, and the welcome message for the Keep Calm Organisers group. This mirrors how [Core recruitment](../core/invite.md) works.

Send the one-pager with the invitation. Someone who accepts the role has seen and accepted the expectations - that protects them and us, and makes any later conversation about the role a reference back to something agreed, not a personal criticism.

---

## The one-pager (send this as a document or link)

### Becoming a Keep Calm Organiser

**The role in one sentence:** you help one activity happen most weeks, alongside your fellow organisers - the systems handle most of the posting, you handle the people and the judgment calls.

**Before anything else: you're a volunteer**

Everyone who helps Keep Calm happen - organisers, admins, Core, all of it - is a volunteer giving up their own free time because they want to. Nothing below is an official rulebook or a performance bar. Think of it as guardrails so we're roughly on the same page, not a checklist you're being marked against. We're all doing this to grow the community together and meet great people in Barcelona along the way.

**What we're hoping for**

- **Reliability, held loosely.** Ideally your sport runs most weeks, between you and your fellow organisers, or a backup - life happens, and that's genuinely fine as long as you let us know.
- **Reachable when you can be**, around your session day - no need to be glued to your phone.
- **Fair with the rules** when it comes up: strikes, levels, and replacements per the [Event Rules](../rules/event-rules.md), the same for friends as for strangers.
- **Welcoming** - you naturally set the tone of your group just by being yourself.
- **A little extra care with conduct**, since people look to organisers for how the group feels. The [Community Conduct](../policies/community-conduct.md) rules apply to you too.
- **Work well with your fellow organisers and admins**, and respect their decisions, even the ones you'd have called differently.

**The bare minimum, if we're honest**

Sessions happen somehow (you, a fellow organiser, or cover), people get answered eventually, and things stay kind. If life gets busy, just say so early - we'll sort cover together, no awkwardness. The only thing that doesn't really work is going quiet.

**What you get**

- Recognition: the organiser title, public thanks, and a community that knows what you do
- Real respect - people notice who makes their sessions happen, and it shows in how they treat you
- A fast-track to [Keep Calm Core](../core/README.md#organiser--admin-fast-track): the role itself already proves most of what Core looks for
- Real input on your sport's direction and the community's direction
- First access to popular and limited events
- Invitations to organiser meetups and socials
- A platform to lead: beginner sessions, new formats, your ideas tried for real
- What we can't offer: money or commercial promotion - Keep Calm is strictly non-commercial, and that applies to organisers too (see [Partnerships & Reciprocity](../rules/partnerships-reciprocity.md))

**If it stops working**

Stepping back is always okay and never held against anyone - most organisers eventually do, and a good handover is all we ask. Removal from the role only happens for sustained unreliability or conduct issues, is decided by the organisers together, and always with the chance to be heard ([Decision Making](decision-making.md)).

**Your day-to-day playbook:** your sport's `organiser-one-pager.md`, the [Organiser Guide](organiser-guide.md), and [Who Does What](raci.md).

## Invitation message (direct message, not in a group)

Replace `[first name]` and `[sport]`, and attach or link the one-pager above.

```
Hi [first name]! 👋

We'd love to invite you to become an *organiser* for Keep Calm [sport] 🌟

*Why you?*
You know the sessions, people know you, and you're exactly the kind of reliable, friendly face this role needs. Organisers are how Keep Calm stays volunteer-run and community-owned.

*What does it involve?*
Less than you might think. Almost all the posting (announcements, reminders, polls, sign-up forms) is automated these days. The organiser's job is the human bit: reviewing the week's games, answering questions, making the day-of calls, and welcoming new people. Most weeks it's 1-3 hours.

*Before you decide*, have a read of the expectations one-pager we're sending with this - it sets out honestly what we'd ask of you, the bare minimum to keep the role, and what you get back. We'd rather you know everything upfront than be surprised later.

*No pressure at all* - it's completely fine to say no, or to ask to co-lead with someone first to try it out.

Any questions, just ask! 🤗
```

## Welcome message (post in the Keep Calm Organisers group)

```
Welcome to the organiser team, [name]! 🎉

[Name] is taking on [sport] - give them a warm welcome 👏

A quick intro so everyone can put a face to the name 👇

👤 Your name and your sport
🌍 Where you're from, and how long you've been in Barcelona
🎾 How you first found Keep Calm
✨ One thing you'd love to try or improve with your sessions

We're all here to help each other, so ask anything anytime - nobody organises alone here. Welcome aboard! 💪
```
