# Role Descriptions

This document defines the roles that keep Keep Calm running. Clear role definitions make it easier to recruit volunteers, set expectations, and ensure continuity when people step back.

---

## Role Overview

| Role | Scope | Time Commitment | Key Responsibility |
|------|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Core Team Member** | Community-wide | 2-4 hrs/month | Strategic direction and cross-sport coordination |
| **Sport Lead** | One sport | 2-3 hrs/week | Owns the sport end-to-end |
| **Session Host** | One session | 1-2 hrs/session | Runs the actual event day |
| **Booker** | One court/session | 30 mins/week | Books and coordinates one game |
| **Beginner Ambassador** | One sport | 1-2 hrs/month | Welcomes and supports newcomers |
| **Social Media Coordinator** | Community-wide | 2-3 hrs/week | Content creation and posting |
| **Event Organiser** | Special events | Varies | Plans and runs non-sport events |

---

## Core Team Member

### Purpose
Provides strategic direction, makes community-wide decisions, and coordinates across sports.

### Responsibilities
- Attend monthly core team meetings
- Make decisions on community policies
- Resolve escalated conflicts
- Approve new sports/major changes
- Support sport leads when needed
- Ensure community health and sustainability

### Requirements
- Deep understanding of Keep Calm values
- Available for monthly meetings
- Responsive to urgent issues
- Experience as Sport Lead or equivalent
- Trusted by the community

### Time Commitment
- Monthly meeting: 1-2 hours
- Async communication: 1-2 hours/month
- Escalated issues: as needed

### This Role is For You If...
- You care about the community beyond any single sport
- You can think strategically and long-term
- You're comfortable making difficult decisions
- You have capacity beyond running your own sport

## Sport Lead

### Purpose
Owns a sport end-to-end. Makes it happen reliably every week.

### Responsibilities
- Post weekly polls/announcements
- Organise games and assign players
- Coordinate bookers and session hosts
- Handle problems and conflicts within the sport
- Onboard new members
- Maintain sport documentation
- Apply strike system for violations
- Recruit and develop helpers
- Report to core team on issues

### Requirements
- Reliable and consistent availability
- Good communication skills
- Understanding of the sport
- Ability to make fair decisions
- Comfortable managing people
- Minimum 3-6 month commitment

### Time Commitment
- Weekly: 2-3 hours
- Event day: Available by phone/WhatsApp
- Occasional: Handover, planning, improvements

### Authority
- Final say on player assignments
- Can assign strikes (within guidelines)
- Can recruit bookers and session hosts
- Can adjust session format/timing
- Major changes need core team approval

### This Role is For You If...
- You love the sport and want it to thrive
- You're organised and can stick to a schedule
- You enjoy helping others have a good experience
- You can handle being the "bad guy" occasionally
- You want to shape how your sport runs

### Backup & Handover
- Should have at least one co-lead or trained backup
- See [Organiser-Guide.md](organiser-guide.md) for handover process

## Session Host

### Purpose
Runs the actual event on the day. The on-the-ground person.

### Responsibilities
- Arrive early to set up (if needed)
- Welcome players as they arrive
- Handle any issues during the session
- Ensure equipment is managed
- Take photos if possible
- Report issues to Sport Lead after
- Thank participants

### Requirements
- Reliable attendance
- Comfortable talking to strangers
- Knows the sport basics
- Can handle minor issues calmly
- Has Sport Lead's contact for emergencies

### Time Commitment
- The session itself: 1-3 hours
- Arrive 10-15 mins early
- Brief follow-up: 10 mins

### Authority
- On-the-ground decisions during session
- Escalates issues to Sport Lead
- Cannot issue strikes (reports to Lead)

### This Role is For You If...
- You're a regular who loves the social aspect
- You're comfortable being a "host"
- You're not ready for the full Lead commitment
- You want to help without the weekly admin

### Rotation
- Can be rotated among regulars
- Sport Lead may assign session by session
- Great stepping stone to Lead role

## Booker

### Purpose
Books and manages one court or session within a larger event.

### Responsibilities
- Book the assigned court promptly (within hours of assignment)
- Share booking link with all assigned players
- Ensure all players join/pay before the session
- Be the point of contact for your court
- Report any issues to Sport Lead
- Handle simple last-minute changes

### Requirements
- Playtomic account (for Padel/Tennis)
- Reliable and responsive
- Available to book promptly when assigned
- Willing to coordinate with 3 other people

### Time Commitment
- Booking: 5-10 minutes
- Coordination: 15-20 minutes over the week
- Day-of: Be available for your court

### Authority
- Coordinates their specific court
- Cannot remove/add players without Lead approval
- Escalates payment issues to Lead

### This Role is For You If...
- You're a regular player who can be counted on
- You don't mind the admin side
- You want a small way to contribute
- You're often assigned to courts anyway

### Assignment
- Sport Lead assigns based on reliability and willingness
- Rotation is fine, but some consistency helps

## Beginner Ambassador

### Purpose
Makes newcomers feel welcome and supported in their first sessions.

### Responsibilities
- Welcome new members when they join the WhatsApp
- Send welcome DM with getting-started info
- Pair newcomers with friendly regulars at their first session
- Check in after first session
- Answer basic questions
- Identify people struggling and connect them with help
- Support monthly beginner sessions

### Requirements
- Friendly and approachable
- Patient with questions
- Knows the basics of the sport
- Good memory for names/faces
- Reliable availability for beginner sessions

### Time Commitment
- Welcome messages: 15-30 mins/week
- First-session check-ins: 15-30 mins/week
- Beginner sessions: 2-3 hours/month
- Can be done alongside regular participation

### Authority
- Informal support role
- Reports feedback to Sport Lead
- Can suggest session improvements

### This Role is For You If...
- You remember being new and want to ease that for others
- You enjoy meeting new people
- You're naturally encouraging
- You have patience for repeated basic questions

## Social Media Coordinator

### Purpose
Manages Keep Calm's social media presence and content.

### Responsibilities
- Collect photos/videos from sessions
- Create and post content (Instagram, Facebook, etc.)
- Maintain consistent posting schedule
- Respond to comments and DMs
- Coordinate member takeovers or spotlights
- Track what content performs well
- Support marketing initiatives

### Requirements
- Familiarity with Instagram/social platforms
- Eye for good photos and content
- Consistent availability to post
- Understanding of Keep Calm voice and values
- Basic photo/video editing skills helpful

### Time Commitment
- Content creation: 1-2 hours/week
- Posting and engagement: 1 hour/week
- Planning: 30 mins/week

### Authority
- Controls what gets posted (within guidelines)
- Can request photos from Sport Leads
- Cannot speak for Keep Calm on controversial topics (escalate to Core Team)

### This Role is For You If...
- You enjoy social media and content creation
- You're already taking photos at events
- You want to contribute beyond playing
- You have a creative eye

## Event Organiser

### Purpose
Plans and runs special events (seasonal parties, trips, cross-sport socials).

### Responsibilities
- Propose and plan the event
- Handle logistics (venue, tickets, food, timing)
- Promote the event across sports groups
- Manage RSVPs and attendance
- Run the event on the day
- Handle money if applicable
- Post-event follow-up

### Requirements
- Organisational skills
- Comfortable handling logistics
- Can work ahead on planning
- Responsive to questions
- Comfortable handling group payments if needed

### Time Commitment
- Varies by event size
- Small social: 2-3 hours total
- Large event (party, trip): 10-20 hours over weeks

### Authority
- Owns their specific event end-to-end
- Sets capacity limits
- Can decline late registrations
- Budget decisions need Core Team approval if spending community funds

### This Role is For You If...
- You enjoy event planning
- You have ideas for things the community could do
- You're good at logistics and details
- You want to bring people together across sports

## Informal Roles

These aren't formal positions but are valuable contributions:

### Regular Helper
- Helps with setup/cleanup
- Brings equipment
- Helps session run smoothly
- First to offer when help is needed

### Equipment Manager
- Tracks what equipment exists
- Maintains balls, nets, etc.
- Sources replacements when needed
- Coordinates equipment for sessions

### Translator
- Helps with venue communications in Spanish/Catalan
- Assists non-English speakers
- Supports international members

### Venue Liaison
- Has relationship with a specific venue
- Handles booking issues
- Knows the venue staff
- Gets info on availability/changes

## Progression Path

Typical journey from newcomer to leader:

```
Newcomer
    ↓ (Attends 5+ sessions)
Regular
    ↓ (Helps out informally)
Booker / Beginner Ambassador
    ↓ (Demonstrates reliability)
Session Host
    ↓ (Takes on more responsibility)
Sport Lead
    ↓ (Proven track record)
Core Team Member
```

Not everyone wants to progress, and that's fine. The community needs reliable regulars as much as it needs leaders.

## Recruiting for Roles

### Identifying Candidates

Look for people who:
- Attend consistently
- Help without being asked
- Are positive and welcoming
- Follow through on commitments
- Get along with different people
- Show interest in how things work

### Making the Ask

1. Thank them for what they already do
2. Explain the role clearly
3. Be honest about the commitment
4. Give them time to think
5. Don't pressure—volunteering must be genuine
6. Provide support when they start

### Supporting New Role Holders

- Pair with experienced person for first few sessions
- Check in regularly in first month
- Be available for questions
- Give feedback (positive and constructive)
- Publicly recognise their contribution
