# Keep Calm "Cool" Meeting - 2 Hour Agenda

A relaxed get-together that does two things at once: helps people genuinely
connect (one-to-one and small-group), and gives everyone a real say in where
Keep Calm goes next so the community feels like theirs.

**Total time:** 2 hours (120 min)
**Who:** ~10-20 people in the room. The 4 Core members host and facilitate;
everyone else (including plus ones) takes part.
**Vibe:** Casual, warm, low-pressure. Drinks/snacks if possible. No slides-heavy
lecturing. The organisers facilitate, they don't present at people.

**Handy maths:** 4 Core members = one facilitator per small group. With 10-20
people you'll have ~4 groups of 3-5, so each Core member naturally takes a
table, keeps the conversation flowing, and captures the notes.

| Time | Block | Type |

## The blocks in detail

### 0:00 - 0:15 - Arrivals & warm welcome (Social)
- Let people trickle in, grab a drink, settle.
- One organiser does a 2-minute genuine welcome: thanks for coming, what tonight
  is *for* ("we want to hang out properly, and we want your honest input on where
  this goes - this is your community").
- Set the tone: relaxed, no wrong answers, phones down where possible.

### 0:15 - 0:30 - Icebreaker round (Social, 1-to-few)
Pick one, keep it light:
- **Two truths and a lie**, or
- **"How did you find Keep Calm and what made you stay?"** in pairs, then each
  person introduces their partner to the group (great for people who don't like
  talking about themselves).
- Goal: everyone has spoken once and learned at least one new name before the
  serious bit.

### 0:30 - 0:50 - Where Keep Calm is today (Light context)
- A short, honest snapshot, not a sales pitch: what's going well, what's grown,
  what's been hard (volunteer load, no-shows, whatever's real).
- Briefly name the vision in plain words: *the sport is the mechanism, belonging
  is the outcome.*
- Frame the ask: "We're not here to tell you the plan. We want to build it with
  you." This sets up the feedback blocks.

### 0:50 - 1:15 - Small-group feedback (Feedback, 1-to-few)
- Split into groups of 3-4 (mix people who don't know each other).
- Each group talks through a few prompts and jots notes on a card/paper:
  - What do you love about Keep Calm right now? Keep doing it.
  - What would you *love* to see us do or try?
  - What's one thing that would make you bring a friend?
  - What direction excites you - more sports? more social? bigger? more
    intimate? more cities?
- Doubles as bonding: small groups = real conversation, not a big intimidating
  circle.

### 1:15 - 1:25 - Break / mingle (Social)
- Refill drinks, switch groups naturally, breathe.

### 1:25 - 1:50 - Share back & prioritise together (Feedback)
- Each group shares their top 1-2 ideas (not everything - keeps it snappy).
- Capture everything visibly (whiteboard, big paper, or a shared note).
- Quick light prioritisation: give everyone 2-3 dot votes (stickers or hands)
  for the ideas they're most excited about. This makes the room's priorities
  visible and shared, so decisions feel collective.

### 1:50 - 2:00 - Wrap, next steps & casual hang (Social)
- Organiser recaps the top themes that emerged and commits to *something*
  concrete: "we'll take these top 3 away and report back."
- Gentle nudge on the ownership path: anyone who wants to help make an idea
  happen, come find us - this is how Keep Calm grows.
- Thank everyone. Then the formal bit ends and people are free to stay and hang.

---

## Facilitator notes
- **Protect the social half.** It's tempting to let feedback eat the whole night.
  Keep a loose eye on time but don't be a drill sergeant.
- **Capture everything.** Assign one person per group to take notes so good ideas
  don't evaporate.
- **Close the loop afterwards.** Post a short summary in the community of what was
  heard and what's happening next. This is what makes people feel genuinely
  invested rather than just "consulted".
- **Mix the groups** deliberately so people meet someone new, not just sit with
  friends.
