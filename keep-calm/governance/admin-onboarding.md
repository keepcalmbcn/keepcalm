# Admin Onboarding - Expectations, Benefits, and Messages

Everything for bringing a new admin on board: the one-pager we send them **before they say yes** (so nothing comes as a shock later), the invitation message, and the welcome message for the admin group. This mirrors how [Core recruitment](../core/invite.md) and [Organiser Onboarding](organiser-onboarding.md) work.

"Admin" covers the people with elevated access and trust across the community: WhatsApp group admin rights, moderation, and community administration. The day-to-day specifics live in the [Moderator One-Pager](moderator-one-pager.md) and [Community Admin One-Pager](community-admin-one-pager.md); this pack is about the expectations that come with the position itself.

Send the one-pager with the invitation. Someone who accepts has seen and accepted the expectations - that makes any later conversation a reference back to something agreed, not a personal criticism.

---

## The one-pager (send this as a document or link)

### Becoming a Keep Calm Admin

**The role in one sentence:** you hold a bit of trust from the community - admin rights exist to keep the groups safe, friendly, and usable, and people should be able to rely on you being fair.

**Before anything else: you're a volunteer**

Admins, like organisers and everyone else who helps Keep Calm happen, are volunteers giving up their own free time because they want to. Nothing below is an official rulebook - it's guardrails so we're roughly on the same page, not a checklist you're being marked against. We're all doing this to grow the community together and meet great people in Barcelona along the way.

**What we're hoping for**

- **A response, not a shrug, when something comes up.** Spam, rule breaches, and especially reports of unsolicited DMs deserve a look. Reports of harassment are the one thing we take seriously without exception ([WhatsApp Rules](../rules/whatsapp-rules.md)).
- **Reachable when you can be.** No need to be online all day - flagged issues getting picked up within a day or so is plenty.
- **Fairness over friendship.** Rules apply the same to friends, regulars, and strangers. If you're too close to a situation, it's totally fine to hand it to another admin.
- **A bit of discretion.** Member issues are best handled privately rather than in the group. What you learn as an admin stays between admins.
- **Keep the access community-only.** Admin rights and member data are for the community, not personal or commercial use - covered by our [Data & Privacy](../policies/data-privacy.md) policy.
- **A little extra care with conduct**, since admins naturally set the example ([Community Conduct](../policies/community-conduct.md)).

**The bare minimum, if we're honest**

Stay reachable-ish, pick up reports within a day or so, act fairly, keep member matters private. If you're travelling or busy, just say so - cover is easy to arrange, going quiet isn't great. Misusing the access or ignoring reports is really the only thing that doesn't work here.

**What you get**

- The trust of the community, and recognition for holding it
- A fast-track to [Keep Calm Core](../core/README.md#organiser--admin-fast-track): the role itself already proves most of what Core looks for
- Real input on rules, policy, and where Keep Calm goes next
- First access to popular and limited events
- Invitations to organiser and admin meetups and socials
- The satisfaction of being why the groups feel safe and friendly
- What we can't offer: money or commercial promotion - Keep Calm is strictly non-commercial (see [Partnerships & Reciprocity](../rules/partnerships-reciprocity.md))

**If it stops working**

Stepping back is always fine and never held against anyone - hand over admin rights and any open threads and you leave with our thanks. Removal only happens for misuse of access, sustained unresponsiveness, or conduct issues, decided by the organisers together, always with the chance to be heard ([Decision Making](decision-making.md)).

**Your day-to-day playbook:** the [Moderator One-Pager](moderator-one-pager.md) and/or [Community Admin One-Pager](community-admin-one-pager.md), plus [Who Does What](raci.md).

## Invitation message (direct message, not in a group)

Replace `[first name]`, and attach or link the one-pager above.

```
Hi [first name]! 👋

We'd love to invite you to join the *Keep Calm admin team* 🛡️

*Why you?*
You're trusted, you're level-headed, and people already come to you when something's off. That's exactly what this role is.

*What does it involve?*
Keeping the WhatsApp groups friendly and safe: welcoming new faces, steering chat back on track, and acting on the rare rule breaches and reports. All the scheduled posting is automated, so this isn't a posting job - it's a judgment and trust job. Little and often, rather than hours at a time.

*Before you decide*, have a read of the expectations one-pager we're sending with this. Admin comes with real responsibility (fairness, discretion, acting on reports), and we'd rather you see exactly what's expected upfront than be surprised later.

*No pressure at all* - it's completely fine to say no or to think it over.

Any questions, just ask! 🤗
```

## Welcome message (post in the admin group)

```
Welcome to the admin team, [name]! 🛡️

[Name] is joining us to help keep the groups running smoothly - give them a warm welcome 👏

A quick intro for everyone 👇

👤 Your name (and what we should call you)
🌍 Where you're from, and how long you've been in Barcelona
🎾 Which Keep Calm activities you're usually at
✨ A fun fact about you

Ask anything here anytime - nobody handles a tricky situation alone, and a second opinion is always one message away. Welcome aboard! 💪
```
