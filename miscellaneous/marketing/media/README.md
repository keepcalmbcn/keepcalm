# Media Guide - Keep Calm Miscellaneous Activities

This folder is for photos and visuals used in marketing Keep Calm's one-off and miscellaneous activities.

## What Works Well

### Group moments
- Candid shots of people trying something new together - the moment before someone has any idea what they're doing is often the best one
- Mixed groups mid-activity, laughing, figuring things out
- Before/after moments - arriving uncertain, leaving with a big smile
- Post-activity hangs: a group around a table, drinks in hand, still buzzing from whatever just happened

### The activity itself
- Action shots from whatever the specific activity is - golf swings, climbing walls, cycling routes, boxing gloves
- The unexpected detail: a scoreboard, a weird piece of equipment, someone's very serious face during a very casual game
- Venues and locations that say "Barcelona" - outdoor settings, good light, recognisable backdrops where possible

### New beginnings energy
- First-timers being shown the ropes
- A small group doing something that clearly hasn't been done before as a Keep Calm event
- "We're making this up as we go and it's going great" energy

## What to Avoid

- Empty venues or activity shots without people
- Overly polished or staged photos - the charm is in the realness
- Photos where people look unsure whether they wanted to be photographed
- Anything that makes the activity look intimidating or exclusive

## Notes for One-Off Events

Since miscellaneous events vary widely, the most useful photos are ones that work across multiple activities: people together, genuine reactions, and the Barcelona setting. Always ask permission before posting photos of identifiable people.
