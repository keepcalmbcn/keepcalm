# Keep Calm Climbing 🧗

Welcome to Keep Calm Climbing! Indoor bouldering and climbing meetups for English speakers in Barcelona, with the occasional trip to the rock outside the city.

Part of the larger **Keep Calm and Carry On** not-for-profit community for English speakers in Barcelona, we're built on values of inclusivity, reliability, and collaboration. Everyone is welcome, and the community belongs to all of us.

## How It Works

- Sessions are posted in the WhatsApp group with the gym, day, time, and entry cost
- If you want to come, respond to confirm so we know numbers
- Turn up at the gym at the stated time and look for the group
- Most sessions are bouldering, which needs no ropes, no partner, and no experience

## What to Expect

- A friendly, mixed-ability group
- Mostly indoor bouldering, with rope climbing when the venue and group suit it
- Newcomers shown the basics by people who have climbed before
- Climbing is social: plenty of resting, chatting, and cheering each other on between problems
- Drinks or food together after some sessions

## Where We Climb

Barcelona has a great spread of climbing gyms. Sessions rotate between venues depending on what the group fancies. Common spots include:

- **Sharma Climbing BCN** (Gavà / Poblenou) - large modern bouldering and rope walls
- **Climbat La Foixarda** (Montjuïc) - bouldering and rope climbing in a former municipal site
- **Other city gyms** - announced per session

Occasional outdoor trips head to crags around Catalonia (for example Montserrat or the Garraf) for those with the experience and gear. Outdoor sessions are always clearly marked and need more preparation.

## Cost

You pay the gym entry directly. Typical indoor entry is around €10-16 for a day pass, depending on the venue. Shoe and (for ropes) harness rental is usually a few euros on top.

- No Keep Calm membership fee
- Bring your own gear if you have it to save on rental
- Exact entry cost is posted with each session announcement

## What to Bring

**For indoor bouldering:**
- Comfortable, stretchy clothing you can move in
- Climbing shoes if you own them (otherwise rent at the gym)
- Water
- A small towel and some chalk if you have it (optional)

**For rope climbing:**
- A harness and belay device if you own them, plus a belay certificate if the gym requires one
- Otherwise rent at the gym and let an experienced member know you are new to ropes

**For outdoor sessions:**
- Proper outdoor gear is essential. Only join outdoor trips if you have the experience or are explicitly partnered with someone who does. Details come with each outdoor announcement.

## New to Climbing?

You are very welcome. Bouldering is the easiest way in:

- No ropes, no partner, no experience needed
- Walls are low and there are thick mats below
- Routes (called "problems") are colour-graded from easy to hard, so you pick your level
- Someone in the group will happily show you how it works

Just turn up to a bouldering session, rent shoes at the desk, and give it a go.

## Safety

- Climb within your limits and warm up first
- For bouldering, learn to fall and land safely - ask an experienced member
- For ropes, never belay unless you know how, and always double-check knots and devices
- Follow all gym rules and staff instructions
- You climb at your own risk - assess your own ability honestly
- Outdoor climbing carries real risk and is only for those properly equipped and experienced
