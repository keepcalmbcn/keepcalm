# Media Guide - Keep Calm Climbing

This folder is for photos and visuals used in marketing the climbing activity.

## What Works Well

### The walls and gyms
- Colourful bouldering walls with the graded holds clearly visible
- Tall rope walls that show the scale of the venue
- Climbers mid-move on an interesting problem
- The mats and open layout that make bouldering feel approachable
- Distinctive gym features (Sharma Climbing BCN and La Foixarda both look great on camera)

### People and group moments
- Candid climbing shots - someone reaching for a hold, not posed
- Small groups gathered at the base of a wall, chatting and watching
- People resting between problems, laughing and cheering each other on
- A beginner being shown the ropes by a more experienced member
- Drinks or food together after a session

### Details
- Climbing shoes and chalk bags
- Hands on holds, chalk dust in the air
- A colour-graded wall close up, showing the range from easy to hard
- The view up a rope wall from the floor

## What to Avoid
- Overly posed or stiff group photos
- Anything that looks like a promotional shoot rather than a real session
- Shots that make climbing look intimidating or only for the very strong - this puts beginners off
- Low-res or heavily filtered shots

## Best Angles for Barcelona Audience
Barcelona has a great spread of climbing gyms within easy reach of the city. Photos that make climbing feel social and welcoming - "anyone can turn up and have a go" - are more compelling than photos that look like a hardcore training session. Show the bouldering side, since that's the easiest way in for newcomers.
