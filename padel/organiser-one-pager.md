# Padel Organiser - One-Pager

**The role in one sentence:** you are the human layer of Sunday Padel - the systems post and build the games, you review them, answer people, and make the judgment calls.

## Weekly rhythm

Sessions run Sundays. Your real touchpoints:

- **Sunday night**: review and sign off the game card for next Sunday when it reaches you
- **During the week**: post the confirmed court assignments and answer questions as they come up
- **Sunday**: be reachable around session time for day-of issues

## Handled automatically (you do nothing)

- Sign-up announcement Thursday evening, reminder Friday evening, last call Saturday evening
- Next week's sign-up form is created automatically on Sunday night
- The game card (courts, levels, bookers) is generated automatically on Sunday night and sent to the reviewer for sign-off
- Post-session thank-you and feedback ask on Sunday evening

## Yours to do

- **Review the game card**: check levels look right, no known conflicts, every court has a booker, then sign off
- **Answer questions** in the group: levels, venues, how the system works
- **Day-of calls**: weather (per the [rain process](padel-rain.md)), no-shows, finding replacements
- **Welcome newcomers**: point them at the [readme](readme.md), help them pick a level, check in after their first session
- **Apply strikes** for no-shows and late cancellations per the [group rules](rules.md)

## You decide without asking anyone

- Player assignments, court/level moves, and session format
- Booker assignments
- Strikes 1 and 2, and replacements for dropouts
- Weather calls, following the [rain process](padel-rain.md)
- Spending up to a session's normal per-player cost (about 8 euros) to solve a problem, e.g. covering a gap on a court ⚙️ CONFIRM

## Decide together with other organisers

- Changing venue or fees, adding or dropping a session
- Strike-3 removals
- Partnerships that touch padel

## Escalate to the Community Lead only for

- Money beyond the above
- Bans and appeals
- Venue contracts
- Anything technical: forms, automation, the website

## Handover

Stepping down? Follow the Stepping Down checklist in the [Organiser Guide](../keep-calm/governance/organiser-guide.md#stepping-down).
