# Absolute Beginners Session – Organiser Guide

This guide covers everything needed to run an Absolute Beginners Padel session. Most of the work happens in the weeks before the event, not on the day itself.

---

## 1. Pre-Event Setup (3–6 Weeks Before)

### Venue
- Book courts at the venue well in advance — aim for 14 courts for a full session
- Confirm the time slot carefully; clashes with other bookings have happened before
- Clarify the per-person court cost (~€6) and racket rental policy (€2 + ID at reception)
- Keep a direct contact at the venue for any last-minute issues

### Recruit Leaders and Helpers
- Create a dedicated "Absolute Beginners Leaders & Helpers" WhatsApp group
- Aim for one leader per court plus a few helpers
- Actively chase — people often need reminders to confirm
- Have a backup ready in case a leader drops out last minute
- See [leader.md](leader.md) and [helper.md](helper.md) for role guides

### Set Up a Shared Source of Truth
- Create a Google Forms and spreadsheet before signups open
- It should contain: court assignments, leader names, player lists, racket counts, confirmed/paid status, and any notes (e.g. Spanish-only speakers, couples who want to play together)
- Share it with all leaders — this is the single source of truth, not the WhatsApp chat
- Keep it updated throughout the confirmation period

### Event Listings
- Create event pages across all platforms: Luma, Heylo, Eventbrite, Google Form, and Playtomic
- Each needs its own copy, pricing, time, and level info
- Write the public description to speak directly to nervous first-timers — lead with reassurance, not logistics
- **Always BCC when emailing all signups** — never use TO

---

## 2. Signup Processing & Court Allocation (1–2 Weeks Before)

This is the biggest invisible job. From the signup form responses you need to:

- Sort players by self-rated skill level
- Group by gender preference where applicable (some courts may be ladies-only)
- Balance players who have rackets vs those who need to rent
- Keep couples and friend pairs together (look for matching phone numbers or comments in the form)
- Match players to leaders based on level and any stated constraints
- Aim for **4 players per court** — do not go to 5 or 6, it causes more stress and degrades the experience

Once allocated, publish the full court list to leaders: each court should show the leader name, level, gender, racket count, and player roster including a waitlist.

### Onboarding Email to Signups
Send all confirmed players an email with:
1. Link to join the event WhatsApp group
2. Instructions to download Playtomic
3. (Optional) invite to join the wider Keep Calm community

---

## 3. Confirmation Period (Days Before)

- Chase leaders regularly for court status updates — do it per-leader, not just a general message to the group
- Go court by court in the final days and verify each player has paid on Playtomic: every court should be fully confirmed before the event
- Manage swaps and edge cases as they come in — update the shared doc for every change
- Manage the waitlist: know who has already paid, who speaks limited English, and who is a priority

### Send Player Communications
Use the templates in [templates/](templates/):

## 4. Day Of

- **Bring balls** — 2 per player per court (easy to forget!)
- Post the final court roster as a pinned message in the WhatsApp group so leaders on the ground can direct lost players
- Handle last-minute arrivals and walk-ins by checking if any court has spare capacity
- At the end of the session, give the organiser speech — see [speech.md](speech.md)

---

## 5. Post-Event

- Thank all leaders and helpers individually — call out specific contributions publicly where you can
- Share any positive player feedback with the relevant leader
- Post the feedback form promptly: [https://keepcalm.fit/feedback](https://keepcalm.fit/feedback)
- Schedule the next session and update any signup forms to point to the new one
- Note down what went wrong and what to improve — update this guide if needed

---

## Key Lessons

- **You will hold a lot of state — don't do it alone.** Use the shared doc and delegate tracking to it
- **4 players per court is the rule.** 5–6 has been tried and is not worth the added stress
- **Leaders need chasing.** Assume nothing is confirmed until you have a yes
- **The writing matters.** Clear, warm communication to players before the event dramatically reduces confusion on the day
