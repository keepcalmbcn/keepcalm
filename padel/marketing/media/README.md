# Padel - Marketing Media Guide

This folder is for storing photos and visuals used in padel marketing. Below are the types of shots that work best.

## Action Shots

- Players mid-rally, especially at the net or going for a smash
- Doubles pairs working together - shows the social/team aspect
- A beginner having a go for the first time - relatable and welcoming
- Close-ups of padel rackets and the distinctive glass-walled courts

## Group and Social Photos

- The post-game group outside the venue (smiling, sweaty, happy)
- The Sunday post-game social at Caña + Tapa - drinks, food, good energy
- Large group shots that show the range of people (ages, backgrounds, levels)
- Candid moments between games - people chatting on court, waiting for the next point

## Venue Shots

- The glass-walled courts from outside - visually distinct and recognisable
- Courts lit up in the evening (Padel Delfos, Up Padel, Aurial all have this)
- Empty court before a session starts - clean, inviting

## What to Avoid

- Overly posed or staged shots
- Photos that make it look elite or competitive rather than welcoming
- Images with no people in them (sport is about community, not just equipment)

## Practical Notes

- Always get consent before posting photos of people
- Natural light shots (Sunday afternoon) tend to look better than heavily filtered ones
- Landscape orientation works best for Facebook and the website; square or portrait for Instagram
- Candid shots of mixed-level groups work better than posed shots of the best players
