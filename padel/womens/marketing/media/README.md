# Media Guide - Keep Calm Women's Padel

This folder is for photos and visuals used in marketing the women's padel activity.

## What Works Well

### Action and play
- Candid in-rally shots - a player mid-swing or at the net, not posed
- Doubles action with both players in frame
- A serve or a smash caught at the right moment
- Beginners getting a clean hit - the look of "I actually did that"

### People and group moments
- Smiles and high-fives between points
- A group shot on court after a session, relaxed and natural
- Players chatting at the side of the court between games
- Candid laughter - showing the relaxed, no-pressure vibe

### Details
- Rackets and balls on the court or a bench
- Borrowed spare rackets laid out - showing you don't need your own gear
- Glass-walled padel courts with good light
- The club exterior or court setting that's recognisably Barcelona

## What to Avoid
- Overly posed or stiff group photos
- Anything that looks like a competitive or pro-level shoot rather than a friendly session
- Shots that make it look intimidating for beginners - this puts people off
- Low-res or heavily filtered shots
- Any image that doesn't make the women-only, all-levels welcome feel clear

## Best Angles for Barcelona Audience
The draw here is friendly, accessible, women-only padel where every level belongs. Photos that feel warm and relaxed - "you'd fit right in here on a first try" - are more compelling than photos that look competitive or exclusive. Make sure visuals reflect a women-only group of mixed ability.
