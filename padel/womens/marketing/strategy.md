# Women's Padel - Growth & Marketing Strategy

How we attract women to Keep Calm Women's Padel and make them feel welcome enough to come back. This is the playbook behind the posts in this folder.

The goal is not just to broadcast that the group exists. It is to remove the specific reasons women hesitate, and replace them with reasons to show up.

---

## The barriers we are removing

Women told us, directly and through the community survey, what holds them back. Every piece of our marketing should answer at least one of these:

1. **"I've never played and I'll look silly."** > We say, every time: all levels welcome, beginners always there, no pressure, people happy to teach.
2. **"I don't own a racket / I don't have the gear."** > Spare rackets can be borrowed. Say it in every post - it removes a real blocker.
3. **"I don't want to turn up alone not knowing anyone."** > Named organiser, clear court and time, and an active bring-a-friend invite.
4. **"It feels intimidating or too competitive."** > Women-only framing, warm candid photos, "supportive not a tournament" language.
5. **"How much does it cost and am I committing?"** > Clear, low per-session court cost (around 8-12 euros), no membership, come when you can.
6. **"Is it safe?"** > Established clubs, named organiser, 18+ adults-only community with clear policies.

If a post does not visibly answer one of these, rewrite it.

---

## The hooks that work for padel

- **Easiest racket sport to fall in love with.** Padel has a famously gentle learning curve. Lead with "you'll be rallying on day one."
- **Borrow-a-racket.** Removes the single most common excuse. Repeat it everywhere.
- **A friendly first door.** The women-only group as the easy way in, with the mixed sessions, americanas, and beginner clinics waiting alongside.
- **Belonging, not competition.** Sell the friendships and the supportive vibe, not the scoreline.

---

## Channels (in priority order)

1. **Within Keep Calm first.** Cross-post to the mixed padel groups, the main community channel, and the women's volleyball group - these women already trust us and are the warmest leads.
2. **Instagram** - feed post + stories. Short reels of real sessions (a beginner landing a clean shot, laughter at the net) outperform static graphics.
3. **Playtomic** - women's sessions and clubs are where active padel players already are. See playtomic-outreach.md for the outreach angle.
4. **Facebook groups** - Barcelona expat / women in Barcelona / English-speakers groups. Follow each group's self-promo rules.
5. **Meetup.com** - listed sessions capture people actively searching for things to do.
6. **Reddit** - r/Barcelona, r/expats_barcelona. Keep it personal and honest, not an advert (see reddit.md).

> Note: when cross-posting to **external** communities that are not ours, keep it neutral and avoid heavy Keep Calm branding where the host group asks for it - respect their rules.

---

## Tactics that actually pull women in

- **Bring-a-friend is the single biggest lever.** Women come and stay when they arrive with someone. Make "bring a friend" a standing line in every post and verbally at every session. A pair of beginners is less daunting than one.
- **A dedicated beginners-welcome session.** Pair the women's group with the [Absolute Beginners](../../absolute-beginners/) format so total first-timers have a no-judgement entry point.
- **Women ambassadors.** A friendly woman who greets newcomers by name at the club converts first-timers into regulars better than any caption.
- **First-timer shout-outs.** Welcome new faces in the group (with their consent) so others see newcomers are normal and celebrated.
- **Partner with women's communities in Barcelona.** Women-in-Barcelona / expat-women networks are a perfect audience. Offer a friendly women-only taster, not a hard sell.
- **Lower the cost of trying.** Borrowable rackets plus a clear modest court cost means a woman can try padel for the price of a coffee. Spell that out.
- **Close the loop.** After a good session, post a warm photo and a "next one is..." - momentum is the best advert.

---

## Content rhythm (lightweight)

- **Before:** session announcement in WhatsApp (poll or Playtomic link) + a story. Reshare 1-2 days out.
- **After:** one warm candid photo or short reel, point to the next session.
- **Monthly:** one "join the women's group" push across Instagram + a Barcelona women's Facebook group.

Keep it sustainable - a steady trickle is better than a burst that fizzles.

---

## What good looks like (rough signals)

- New women joining the WhatsApp group month on month
- First-timers returning for a second session (the real test)
- Women bringing friends
- Women moving into the mixed sessions and americanas when they feel ready
- Women stepping up to host or organise - the ultimate sign it's working, and the path to the group outlasting any one person

---

## Tone reminders

- Warm, plain, human. No corporate or salesy language.
- All-levels-welcome and women-only must be unmistakable in every asset.
- Never overstate - if a session is small and chilled, say so. Honesty is the brand.
- No em dashes anywhere (community style rule).
