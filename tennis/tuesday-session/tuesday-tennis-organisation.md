## Booking Tips

### Why the two-week cycle matters
Up Cornell√† has a 7-day booking window. By posting the event two weeks ahead, we collect the Going list one week before the game ‚Äî and then book the court that Tuesday evening after tennis. This means courts are secured 6‚Äì7 days out, which is when the best slots are still available. Waiting until the week of the game (or even Monday that week) often leaves only late slots or nothing at 19:30/20:30.

### Quick court vs clay
- **Quick court** (hard): ‚Ç¨31.20 for 90 min ‚Üí ‚Ç¨7.80 each. Cheaper and usually more available. Shoes don't get dirty.
- **Clay**: ‚Ç¨39 for 90 min ‚Üí ‚Ç¨10 each. Better for technique, but shoes get orange dust on them. Some players prefer it.
- When in doubt, book quick court ‚Äî it's cheaper and the difference in play isn't significant at our level.

### When courts are only available at 20:30
This is common ‚Äî accept it rather than cancelling. Players generally prefer a late game to no game. Just communicate the time clearly so everyone can plan their evening.

### Two courts
If you have 8+ confirmed players, try to book two courts at the same time. Having two people try to book simultaneously (one books court A, one books court B) works well. Courts go fast so don't wait for one to confirm before the other books.

### Court split by level
When organising two courts, try to balance:
- Evenly matched players make for better games
- Aim for one court per skill tier when possible
- In practice: if you know players' levels, group them; otherwise just split evenly

## Managing Dropouts

### Before the game (5+ days)
- Player tells the group, tries to find a replacement
- If no replacement: 3-person court is still possible (one person sits out each game/rotates)

### Last minute (under 24 hours)
- Post urgently in the group looking for a replacement
- If confirmed player drops out and can't find replacement, they may owe their share per group rules (see [rules.md](../rules.md))
- Cancellation at Up Cornell√† costs ‚Ç¨0.50 if done before 10:00 on the day

### No-show
- Treat as a strike per group rules
- Message privately first to understand circumstances before escalating

## Growing the Group

Tuesday Tennis is still a young group. A few things that help:

- **Consistency**: A game every week (even a small one) builds habit and attracts regulars
- **Posting outcomes**: A photo or quick recap after a session builds energy and encourages others to join
- **Welcoming newcomers**: The first time someone joins is when they decide if they'll come back ‚Äî make it easy
- **Flexibility on venue**: Some members can't make Cornell√† regularly. Rotating to Vall d'Hebron occasionally brings in people who wouldn't otherwise join
