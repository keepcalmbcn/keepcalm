# Media Guide - Keep Calm Languages

This folder is for photos and visuals used in marketing the languages activity.

## What Works Well

### Venue and atmosphere
- Warm, inviting bar or cafe interiors with people in conversation
- Tables with drinks, where the focus is clearly on chatting
- Terrace or outdoor seating with a relaxed evening feel
- Cosy corners that look approachable rather than formal

### People and group moments
- Candid shots of pairs or small groups deep in conversation
- People laughing mid-chat, showing the friendly, low-pressure vibe
- A wider shot of the group spread across a few tables
- Someone helping another person find a word - patience and encouragement in action

### Details
- A notebook or phone with new words jotted down
- Drinks on the table, glasses clinking
- A mix of faces and ages, showing all levels and backgrounds are welcome
- Subtle touches of Spanish or Catalan (a menu, a sign) to set the scene

## What to Avoid
- Anything that looks like a classroom, lecture, or formal lesson
- Overly posed or stiff group photos
- Photos where people look bored, awkward, or put on the spot
- Low-res or heavily filtered shots
- Anything that suggests you need to already be "good" to take part

## Best Angles for Barcelona Audience
The appeal is how easy and unintimidating it is. Photos that feel like "a relaxed evening with a drink and good company" land better than anything that looks like study or hard work. Show the social side first and the language practice will sell itself.
