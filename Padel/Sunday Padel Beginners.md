# Absolute Beginners Padel Session Guide

## Overview
- **Venue**: Aurial Pàdel Cornellà by Marta Marrero
- **Frequency**: Aim to run one beginner session per month (ideally first week of month)
- **Timing**: Leave a 30-60 minute break between beginner session and regular padel session
- **Equipment**: 2-4 balls per court (Dan will aim to bring these or players can bring their own)

## Pre-Event Setup

### Registration Form (Google Form/Event Platform)
Ask participants:
- Do they have a racket or will they need to borrow one?
- Do they have experience in padel/tennis or other sports?
- Is there another player they want to play with (partner)?

**Note**: Racket rental is a special deal at 2 euros instead of 4 - rent on the day at reception, not through Playtomic.

### Volunteer Coordination
- Send messages to the group to gather volunteers for organization
- Find one volunteer leader per court to teach/help
- Give each leader a teaching plan of things to cover
- Instruct leaders to get players into the WhatsApp group

### Pre-Event Communications
- Send welcome email with WhatsApp link to all attendees
- Post "how to play padel" and basic rules video
- Remind participants: Ideally bring own racket or borrow from a friend. If they play more in future, it's worth buying from Decathlon.
- Inform about racket rental special offer (2 euros at venue)

## Day of Event

### Arrival & Setup
- Agree on a meeting location at the start
- Players must go to reception and pay for their place
- Players can rent a racket for 2 euros at reception (bring ID as they may ask for it)

### During the Session
- Leaders should ensure player experience is good and minimize players standing around
- Make sure games are set to non-competitive and scores are not uploaded

### After the Session
- Gather for a group photo (will be used for social media and promoting the next beginner session)
- Meet at agreed location after games
- Thank everyone for coming and thank volunteers for volunteering their time
- Tell everyone about Keep Calm and the wider community

## Post-Event Follow-Up
- Thank all volunteers and players
- Setup and post feedback form
- Schedule next absolute beginner session (e.g., for January) and point old form to new form


## Teaching Guide for Volunteer Leaders

### Key Responsibilities
- Understand player experience is the priority
- Minimize players standing around
- Ensure rotation and engagement

### Topics to Cover with Beginners
1. **How to hold the racket** - Proper grip technique
2. **How to serve** - Basic serving technique and rules
3. **The basic rules** - Scoring, faults, let serves
4. **Court zones** - Where you can/cannot play from
5. **Playing off the glass/metal** - How the walls work in padel
6. **Point system** - How scoring works in padel
7. **When to move/switch sides** - Court positioning and rotation
