# Keep Calm Watersports - Group Rules

Welcome to Keep Calm Watersports! To keep things safe and enjoyable for everyone, please follow these guidelines.

> **These rules add to - but don't replace - our community-wide policies. Please read these first if you haven't already:**
> - [WhatsApp Rules](https://github.com/keepcalmbcn/keepcalm/blob/main/keep-calm/rules/whatsapp-rules.md) - group communication, unsolicited DMs, referral links, spam
> - [Community Conduct](https://github.com/keepcalmbcn/keepcalm/blob/main/keep-calm/policies/community-conduct.md) - respect, harassment, advertising, conflict resolution
> - [Event Rules](https://github.com/keepcalmbcn/keepcalm/blob/main/keep-calm/rules/event-rules.md) - commitment, cancellations, the strike system
> - [Safety & Liability](https://github.com/keepcalmbcn/keepcalm/blob/main/keep-calm/policies/safety-liability.md) - participation at your own risk, the 18+ age requirement
>
> **You must be 18 or over to take part. Keep Calm is an adults-only community.**

## 1. Commitment and Payment

- Spots are usually limited and often prepaid to secure a real venue booking
- Only sign up if you genuinely plan to attend
- If you can't make it, tell the organiser as early as possible so your spot can go to someone else
- If a spot has been booked and paid for on your behalf, you may still owe your share if you cancel late - see [Payments, Refunds & Cancellation](../keep-calm/policies/payments-refunds-cancellation.md)

## 2. Swimming and Water Confidence

- Water activities assume a reasonable level of swimming confidence unless the event says otherwise
- Be honest with the organiser if you're not a confident swimmer - most venues can still accommodate you safely, but they need to know
- Life vests or other safety equipment provided by the venue must be worn if instructed

## 3. Safety

- Follow the venue or instructor's safety briefing and instructions at all times
- Check conditions (wind, currents, weather) before going out - the organiser or venue makes the final call on whether it's safe to proceed
- Stay within the group and the area shown to you, don't go off alone
- Tell the organiser if you're struggling, feeling unwell, or unsure about anything
- This is a physical activity at open water: everyone participates independently and is responsible for their own safety, fitness, and decisions. Organisers arrange access but are not responsible for injuries

## 4. Sun and Water Exposure

- Bring water, sunscreen, and something to cover up with before and after
- Barcelona sun and glare off the water are stronger than they look, even at sunset

## 5. Respect for Venues and Equipment

- Follow the venue's rules and the instructor's guidance
- Treat boards, paddles, and any borrowed equipment with care
- Good relationships with venues keep these events possible - be a good guest

## 6. Be Sound

- All abilities welcome, be patient and encouraging with first-timers
- Keep it social, keep it fun, and help the organiser keep an eye on the group
